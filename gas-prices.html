Tracking Gasoline and Diesel Prices

NORC’s weekly surveys provide steady data for the U.S. Energy Information Administration

Being able to understand and forecast changes in fuel costs is an important planning need for a variety of businesses and industries. As subcontractor of Z, Inc., NORC conducted the EIA-878 “Motor Gasoline Price Survey” and EIA-888 “On-Highway Diesel Fuel Price Survey” for the U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) from April 2009 to October 2017.

The average price of U.S. retail gasoline rose $.28/gallon the week after Hurricane Harvey hit Houston.

The weekly surveys gathered data on the cash price of self-serve, conventional, and reformulated motor gasoline and self-serve, motor vehicle diesel fuel from a nationally representative panel of gas stations. In 2017, the Motor Gas Price Survey found that the U.S. experienced its second-lowest summer gas prices since 2009, and prior to Hurricane Harvey, gas production levels were running at near record highs.
