Partisanship and the Media


The Media Insight Project, a collaboration between the American Press Institute and The AP-NORC Center, investigates how Americans of different partisan stripes get news and their attitudes toward the news media.

Click on the buttons to learn more about the findings

There are 100 blue circles (Democrat), 100 red circles (Republican), and 100 white circles (Independent). Each circle represents 1/100, or 1%, for each political party. For example, one red circle represents 1% of respondents identifiying as belonging to the Republican Party; six blue circles represents 6% of respondents who are Democrat
